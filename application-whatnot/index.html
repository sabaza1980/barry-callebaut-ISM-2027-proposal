The Case for International

Whatnot's biggest unlock
isn't in the US.

You've crossed $6B in GMV, doubled year-on-year, hit #1 Shopping in both the US and UK. The infrastructure is proven. The model works. The next chapter isn't just Canada, France, and Germany — it's Europe-wide, the Middle East, Latin America, and beyond. That scale requires a different kind of product thinking.

Europe
Dozens of markets, most barely touched
UK, France, Germany are live — but the Netherlands, Spain, Italy, Poland, Scandinavia, and a dozen more markets are waiting. Europe is not one market. It's 30+ with different languages, payment behaviours, and category cultures. The opportunity is enormous and the playbook doesn't exist yet.
Playbook
The repeatable international model doesn't exist yet
Every market Whatnot enters today requires bespoke investment. The opportunity is to build a repeatable expansion playbook — standardised buyer/seller onboarding, local trust architecture, and market health scorecards that make the next launch 3x faster.
Hub & Spoke
International shouldn't be a fork of the US product
Every "international feature" built as a one-off fork creates long-term debt. The unlock is building global-ready foundations: i18n by default, localisation as a configuration layer, not a rebuild.
MENA + LatAm
The markets nobody's mapped yet
The Middle East has massive collector culture, high mobile commerce adoption, and deep passion for categories Whatnot already owns. Latin America — Brazil, Mexico, Argentina — is live commerce native. These are not distant future markets. They're the next wave, if someone builds the right entry strategy.

"Every new market is its own company. Same infrastructure, but different language, different regulations, different cultural moments. The secret is scalable processes that flex for local relevance — so each expansion moves faster than the last."

How I'd Approach It

The international growth
framework I'd bring.

Four principles I've used to scale marketplaces internationally — and that map directly to what Whatnot needs to do in UK, France, Germany, Canada, and Australia.

01
Measure First

Define healthy before you optimise

Before any experiment, I'd establish what "good" looks like per market — acquisition, activation (first transaction within N days), engagement (repeat buying, show attendance), and monetisation (GMV per active user). These become the shared language between PM, regional teams, and leadership, and the basis for every prioritisation decision. One clear definition of health beats ten competing opinions.

02
Systemic Unlocks

Find the chronic blockers before the paper cuts

In each market, I'd identify the 2–3 friction points that block the most users from reaching their first valuable moment — whether that's payment trust in Germany, category depth in France, or logistics speed in the UK. One systemic fix beats ten paper cuts. I'd size each opportunity in GMV impact before any engineering commitment.

03
Hub & Spoke

Global foundations, local configuration

Every localisation should be a configuration layer on top of a scalable platform — not a fork. I'd work with Engineering and Design to bake i18n, regulatory constraints, and local UX requirements into the core product from the start. This means international features ship faster, degrade more gracefully, and compound across markets rather than fragmenting the codebase.

04
Playbook Machine

Make each market launch faster than the last

Every new market we enter teaches us something. I'd systematise those learnings — regional launch templates, seller onboarding playbooks, local trust-building sequences — so that the fifth market we launch is 3x faster and cheaper than the first. The goal is a repeatable international expansion engine, not a series of one-off projects.

First 90 Days

What I'd actually do.

Not a generic onboarding plan. A sequenced set of moves designed to deliver the first scorecard, the first systemic unlock, and the first cross-market experiment within 90 days.

Days 1 – 30
Understand the machine
  • Deeply inside every international market — talk to 20+ sellers and buyers in UK, France, Germany, Canada, Australia
  • Shadow regional CatEx, Ops, and Marketing — understand where the friction is, not just what the data says
  • Audit existing country-level metrics and identify gaps in measurement coverage
  • Map the current international product surface: what's localised, what's forked, what's missing
  • Identify the single biggest chronic blocker per market — the one thing that is costing the most GMV
Days 31 – 60
Build the foundation
  • Publish the first version of country-level health metrics with agreed definitions across leadership, regional teams, and Data Science
  • Prioritise the international roadmap for the next two quarters: systemic unlocks ranked by GMV impact, paper cuts ranked by fix cost
  • Design the first cross-market experiment: one hypothesis that can be tested simultaneously across 2–3 markets
  • Draft the international hub-and-spoke architecture proposal with Engineering and Design — what needs to be built at platform level vs. configured locally
  • Align with regional leads on a shared cadence: weekly country scorecard reviews, monthly roadmap syncs
Days 61 – 90
Ship the first thing
  • First systemic unlock live in at least one market — impact measured against agreed targets
  • First cross-market experiment running — activation funnel or seller onboarding improvement
  • International playbook v1 drafted: a launch template that makes the next new market faster
  • Present the 12-month international roadmap to leadership — grounded in scorecard data, sequenced by impact
  • Begin building the case for "global-ready by default" in the core product development process
