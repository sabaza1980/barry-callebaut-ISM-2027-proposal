The Plan

First 100 Days

Day one thinking. Three phases. One goal: build a partnership machine with global reach and the local depth to make MrBeast a household name in every market it enters.

Days 1–30
LISTEN & MAP
  • Personally in every room — I don't delegate the listening phase. I'm at every meeting, every table, with every existing partner and internal team member. The best intelligence doesn't filter through layers. I show up for it directly.
  • Deep partnership audit — understand every existing deal structure, brand relationship, and internal process from the inside. What's working, what isn't, and what's being left on the table.
  • Build the global opportunity map — not just what we have, but what's missing. Which categories are unclaimed? Which markets have no local brand champion? What does "household name" mean in Lagos, Lyon, Lima, and Los Angeles — and is MrBeast hitting that bar everywhere?
  • Team alignment — meet every person in the partnerships, production, and creative chain. Understand exactly how deals flow and where the friction lives before touching anything.
  • Activate the network immediately — I arrive with existing relationships across FMCG, food & beverage, entertainment, and sport in 80+ countries. Within 30 days, I'm already in warm conversations — not building a contacts list from scratch.
Days 31–70
BUILD & PITCH
  • Build the Partnership Machine — a scalable, modular framework with global standards and built-in local flexibility. Not one-size-fits-all: a system regional markets can activate, adapt, and own so deals happen in parallel, not in sequence, and MrBeast can grow everywhere at once.
  • Map the aspiration gap — identify where MrBeast has recognition but not yet aspiration. The markets where the brand is known but not yet felt as native. Build the partnership strategy that closes that gap: the right brands, the right local cultural signals, the right content formats for each region.
  • Open the first warm conversations — starting with partners where I have existing trust: Barry Callebaut (supply chain relationship, not a cold call), Brand Abu Dhabi through Saleh Al Jaziry (a call, not a pitch deck), and the NBA through my prior work on their European and African launches.
  • Build the pitch infrastructure — templated decks, media kits, audience data packages, and regional market briefs that make "yes" the easiest answer in any room, on any continent.
  • Close the first landmark deal — a partnership that signals a new standard: global in ambition, locally activated, built to generate content the audience loves wherever they are in the world.
Days 71–100
SCALE & WIN
  • Prove the machine works — take the framework from theory to evidence. Show it produces results at both scales: a landmark global deal and a meaningful regional activation in at least one non-US market, both closed within 100 days.
  • Launch the local champion network — identify regional leads or agency partners in MENA, Europe, Latin America, and Asia-Pacific who can run the playbook in their markets without HQ bottlenecks. Global reach through local ownership, not global management of local details.
  • Build aspiration into every deal — each new partnership should make MrBeast feel more like a household name in that market. The right cultural hooks, the right local co-creation, content that makes audiences feel like MrBeast was built for them — not just exported to them.
  • Turn partners into advocates — structured partner reviews and creative collaboration sessions that convert first deals into multi-year relationships and generate warm referrals to the next partner.
  • Present the 12-month vision — a data-grounded roadmap for where brand partnerships take MrBeast globally next: built on what I've learned inside, who I know outside, and what the audience responds to everywhere.

NBA
🇺🇸 US Core · Sport & Entertainment
NBA
National Basketball Association · 30 teams · 1.8B global fans · The most Gen Z-indexed major league in America
"Two of the biggest Gen Z entertainment properties in America — and they haven't collided yet at scale."
The Opportunity

MrBeast's core US audience and the NBA's fastest-growing fan segment are the same demographic. Both are in a race to own Gen Z's long-term cultural attention — and neither has found the other at the scale either deserves. The existing MrBeast connection to the league is the starting point. The real opportunity is a multi-pillar, multi-year partnership that makes the relationship structural, not episodic — content, live events, and Feastibles woven through the entire NBA calendar year.

Content Concepts
  • "Last Fan in the Arena" — MrBeast-format endurance event inside an NBA arena. Full league branding. The drama is already built in
  • Player Access Series — challenge videos with active NBA stars at practice facilities. Real players, real stakes, organic virality
  • Step × NBA Financial Literacy — co-branded content targeting teen fans. "How NBA rookies manage their first paycheck." Deepens Beast Financial's US cultural relevance at exactly the right age
  • Beast Games × NBA Format — an amateur basketball competition season with NBA-adjacent branding and $1M+ stakes. A content vertical neither side could build alone

Barry Callebaut
World's largest B2B chocolate manufacturer · 60+ countries · 25%+ of global chocolate supply · Already inside Feastibles
"They're already in the Feastibles supply chain. The relationship exists at B2B level. The brand story doesn't — yet."
The Opportunity

Barry Callebaut already supplies the chocolate inside Feastibles. The relationship exists at the B2B level — the brand story doesn't, yet. "Where does Feastibles chocolate actually come from?" is a question that could generate millions of views, elevate the product into a premium tier, and give Barry Callebaut its first meaningful consumer-facing narrative. The supply chain partnership becomes the content. Neither side has to invent a connection — they just have to tell the one that already exists.

Content Concepts
  • "I Went to the Factory Where Feastibles is Made" — a full production tour at Barry Callebaut's Belgian or Swiss facility. The "how it's made" video MrBeast's audience has never seen for their favourite chocolate
  • Feastibles Reserve — Origin Collection — co-developed limited edition SKU using single-origin Grand Cru cacao. Higher price point, limited run, dedicated content launch. Proof that Feastibles can own the premium tier
  • "Bean to Bar Challenge" — can MrBeast's team make chocolate from raw cacao in 24 hours? Barry Callebaut as technical expert and judge. The video writes itself
  • Sustainability Narrative — "Forever Chocolate" meets Beast Philanthropy. Joint ESG storytelling: world's most viral food brand + most sustainably committed chocolate manufacturer
