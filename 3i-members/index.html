The Approach

Begin with the feeling.

Before a venue, a speaker or a single line of the run-of-show, I ask one question: how should a member feel when they walk out of this room? Pride at the company they keep. The quiet thrill of a deal only heard here. The ease of a host who anticipated everything. The feeling is the brief — everything else is in service of it.

That instinct is what turns programming into an identity rather than a calendar. Themes connect, rooms build on one another, and a member starts to feel that 3i is the table they cannot afford to miss.

My First 30 Days

Listen, define, then build the pipeline.

Days 1–10Listen & immerse

Understand the room before changing it

  • Sit with the SVP, regional directors and the three coordinators — learn how the machine runs and where it strains.
  • Meet members across the spectrum — founders, family-office principals, fund managers — and ask what a 3i room makes them feel today.
  • Audit the last year of events and the live calendar: what landed, what felt flat, what people still talk about.
Days 11–20Define the identity

Shape a programming point of view

  • Translate member insight and cultural trends into a thematic arc for the coming quarter — sharp, connected, worth clearing a calendar for.
  • Define the feeling each format should create — the intimate dinner, the regional salon, the speaker series, the summit.
  • Draft a blueprint for success: repeatable, scalable event concepts and production standards the team can run again and again, in any market.
Days 21–30Build the pipeline & begin

Identify, partner, and start executing

  • Identify and prioritise the first pipeline of events — the rooms worth producing first, and the feeling each is built to create.
  • Identify and lock the partners, venues and suppliers each will need — drawing on a global bench already in place — and begin execution.
  • Stand up the operating rhythm: pre-event briefings, run-of-show discipline, and honest post-event debriefs that raise the bar each time, and present the quarter's vision and blueprint to leadership.

Through all of it, one test holds: every choice has to answer how it makes a member feel — because that is the only thing they will remember, and the reason they will come back.
