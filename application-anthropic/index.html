Event Philosophy
How I think about brand events.

Every event I design starts from the same question: what should people believe that they didn't believe before they walked in?

01
Brand First, Production Second
The creative brief isn't "what does this look like?" — it's "what does this need to make people feel?" Every spatial, programming, and experiential decision flows from the brand's core conviction. For Anthropic, that conviction is that safe AI is possible.
02
Audiences Are Stakeholders
Different rooms need different programming. A developer night is not a policy roundtable is not a media briefing — even if they all happen at the same conference. I design every touchpoint around who's in the room and what they need to leave believing.
03
Process Creates Freedom
The best events happen when the production engine is so tight that the creative team can focus on quality. Playbooks, checklists, run-of-show discipline — not because I love process, but because it's what protects the creative ambition when things go sideways on the day.
04
Measure What Matters
Attendance numbers are vanity metrics. What matters is: did this shift perception? Did the right people have the right conversations? Did coverage reflect the brand narrative we intended? I build measurement frameworks around outcomes, not outputs.

First 90 Days
How I'd start.

Three phases: understand the brand deeply, audit the event programme, then start building the machine.

Days 1–30 · Listen & Map
Get inside the brand
  • Immerse in Anthropic's brand voice, values, and messaging pillars — every public talk, paper, and communication
  • Meet brand creative, comms, policy, and product teams — understand how they see the events function
  • Audit the existing event calendar — what's worked, what hasn't, and why
  • Map the full stakeholder landscape: internal owners, key vendors, priority conference relationships
  • Identify the 1–2 events in the next 90 days that will define the baseline
Days 31–60 · Diagnose & Design
Set the standard
  • Define the Anthropic event brief template — brand conviction first, logistics second
  • Establish measurement framework: what does success look like beyond attendance?
  • Produce or sharpen the next owned event with the new standard applied end-to-end
  • Identify the priority conference and sponsorship moments for the next 12 months
  • Begin auditing vendor and agency relationships — who stays, who gets replaced
Days 61–90 · Build & Scale
Build the machine
  • Deliver the first event to the new standard — and debrief rigorously
  • Publish the first version of the Event in a Box toolkit for partners and internal teams
  • Draft the 12-month event calendar with rationale for each moment — owned, sponsored, and partner
  • Establish quarterly review cadence with brand, comms, and leadership
  • Begin building the full-time team structure around the programme needs
